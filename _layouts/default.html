Jiangmiao Pang

Hi! I am currently a Research Scientist at Shanghai AI Laboratory, led by Prof. Dahua Lin. I obtained my Ph.D. degree from Zhejiang University in 2021 and did my postdoc at MMLab, CUHK afterwards. During my postgraduate study, I was fortunate to work with Dr. Jianping Shi at SenseTime (2017-2019), Prof. Fisher Yu and Prof. Trevor Darrell at UC Berkeley (2019-2020), and Prof. Dahua Lin at MMLab (2020-2022).

My research interests span computer vision, machine learning, and robotics. My long-term research goal is to build intelligent systems that can perceive and act like human beings. Currently, my team is focusing on multi-modal 4D perception and exploring its connections with robot learning, on various robotic platforms. NOTE: We have open positions include full-time/part-time researchers, engineers, and joint-training PhD students. If you are interested in joining us, contact me directly!

I am also part-time affiliated with OpenMMLab as a core contributor, where I lead/co-author several visual perception codebases, mainly including MMDetection , MMTracking , and MMDetection3D . My works have won 1st prizes at COCO Object Detection Challenge 2018 and 2019 (without external data), and 1st runner up at Waymo 2D Object Tracking Challenge 2020.
